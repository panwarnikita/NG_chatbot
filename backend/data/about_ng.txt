### 🏢 1. What is NavGurukul?
NavGurukul is a technology-driven non-profit organization based in Gurugram, Haryana, India, dedicated to empowering underserved youth — with a strong focus on women and girls — through high-quality, fully-funded tech education. It offers residential programs that train students in software engineering, design, media, finance, and management, with a guaranteed pathway to aspirational employment in the IT sector.
NavGurukul operates as a meaningful alternative to conventional college education. Students from humble economic backgrounds receive free residential training, food, and learning resources, and are only expected to pay back a nominal fee (in installments) once they secure a job.


## 🎯 2. Mission and Vision
Mission: To provide underserved youth — especially women and girls from rural India, scheduled castes and tribes, and economically disadvantaged communities — free, high-quality education and skills training that creates pathways to aspirational, well-paying careers.
Vision: To create an equitable higher education alternative where students from underserved communities can learn skills to build aspirational careers and thrive in the world. NavGurukul envisions a world where affordable, world-class education empowers every individual to reach their highest potential.




## 👨‍💻 3. Founder of Navgurukul
NavGurukul was co-founded by:
Abhishek Gupta: An alumnus of IIT Delhi, Forbes India 30 Under 30. Abhishek had previously co-founded two startups before volunteering with Delhi government’s education department. Witnessing firsthand how talented students from humble backgrounds lacked access to quality education motivated him to build NavGurukul.



## 💡 5. What Problem Does NavGurukul Solve?
NavGurukul addresses several interconnected problems in India’s higher education and employment landscape:

Access Gap: Quality higher education is a distant dream for youth from rural India, scheduled castes/tribes, religious minorities, and economically disadvantaged families.

Employability Crisis: Conventional colleges often fail to deliver practical skills. Thousands of graduates remain unemployed because they lack market-relevant capabilities.

Gender Inequality in Tech: Women from marginalized backgrounds face extra economic and social barriers.

Digital Poverty: Lack of devices and internet. NavGurukul addresses this through Meraki and Sama (refurbishing laptops).

NavGurukul solves these problems through:

Fully-funded, free residential campuses (no cost to learners)

A peer-learning, self-paced model with no traditional teachers or exams

Open-source learning platform (Meraki) accessible via smartphone

Income-sharing model: students pay back only after securing jobs

English language and personality development training

Programs like: School of Second Chances, Zuvy, Code India Fellowship, and AI-powered tools.





## 🎓 NavGurukul Campus Pedagogy
NavGurukul follows a modern and student-centered learning pedagogy that focuses on practical skills, self-learning, and community-based education. The goal is to help students become independent learners, strong problem solvers, and job-ready professionals.

1. Self-Learning (Student-Driven Learning)
Students learn through online learning platforms, projects, and exercises. Instead of traditional classroom teaching, students take responsibility for their own learning.

Example:

Students study coding modules online

They practice assignments daily

They solve problems on their own first

2. Peer Learning
Students learn from each other.

Example:

If one student understands a concept well, they help others.

3. Mentorship Support
Mentors and facilitators are available to guide students when they get stuck.

Example:

They support students in career preparation

4. Project-Based Learning
Students learn by building real projects.

Example:

Web development projects

This helps students gain practical experience.

5. Holistic Development
NavGurukul focuses not only on coding but also on overall personality development.
Students learn:

Teamwork

6. Community Living
Students live together in the campus which helps them learn:

### 🔄 Re-Test Information (Detailed)
1. Purpose of the Re-Test
The re-test is provided to give students another opportunity if they were unable to clear the Screening test (previous) test or want to improve their performance. It helps students prepare better and demonstrate their learning ability.

2. When Can a Student Take a Re-Test?
A student can request a re-test if:

They did not pass the Pre-Test / ST Test

They missed the test due to technical issues

They want to improve their score

They were unable to complete the test due to network or device problems

3. Re-Test Eligibility Criteria
Students may be eligible for a re-test if they:

Completed the first test honestly

Show interest in continuing the admission process

Follow the admission team instructions

4. Re-Test Topics
The re-test will include questions from similar topics such as:

Math: Patterns, Percentages, Profit and Loss, Simple Interest, Time and Work.

Reasoning: Logical reasoning, Basic problem solving.

5. Preparation Tips for Students
Before taking the re-test, students should:

Practice basic math questions

Solve pattern and reasoning problems

Revise previous test topics

Ensure stable internet connection and proper device

6. Re-attempt for LR Scheduling
Receiving a test link through WhatsApp or email.

7. Re-Test Rules
Students must follow these rules:

Do not take help from others

Do not use external resources during the test

Complete the test within the given time

Maintain honesty and integrity

8. What Happens After the Re-Test?
After completing the re-test:

The admission team will review the results

If the student clears the test, they will move to the next round (LR Round)

If not selected, the student may apply again in future after few days

### GROUP: ABOUT NAVGURUKUL (OVERVIEW & MISSION)
NavGurukul is a technology-led non-profit organization established in 2016 with a mission to enable underserved youth across India to access dignified, future-ready careers. Founded on the radical belief that "Talent is universal, but opportunity is not," the organization works to dismantle structural barriers that prevent millions of young people from realizing their potential. By designing inclusive and scalable systems, NavGurukul provides high-quality, affordable, and outcomes-driven learning pathways that prepare students—often first-generation learners—for the growing digital economy.

- Core Purpose: Bridges the divide between potential and opportunity through industry-relevant education and skilling.
- Strategic Methods: Promotes digital literacy, quality higher education, and improved employment access.
- Long-term Goal: To create a nationwide network where education and skills are inclusive and accessible to every motivated student.
- Official website link of Navgurukul - https://www.navgurukul.org/

## 💰 Fee Structure of NavGurukul Programmes

Most programmes at NavGurukul are fully funded. This means students do not have to pay any course fees.  

The following schools are fully funded programmes:
- School of Education  
- School of Programming  
- School of Business  
- School of Finance  
- School of Second Chance  

Students who join these schools do not need to pay any fees, as the programme is supported and funded by NavGurukul. It’s a scholarship you can get after clearing the test and interviews through the admission process.  

---

## 🎓 BCA Programme Fee

The BCA programme has a one-time fee of ₹23500.  

Students only need to pay this one-time amount, and the rest of the learning support is provided through NavGurukul.  

The fee component is included to ensure the commitment of the students. If a student drops out midway through the course, the seat remains vacant for the entire academic year, since this is a degree program. Unlike diploma or certificate programs, where admissions can happen on a rolling basis, degree program seats cannot be filled once the academic year has started.  

---

## 💻 Laptop Support

Students are provided with laptops on campus for the duration of the course.  

No personal laptop is required to start the application or the selection process.  

---

## 🚀 Selection Process

- Online/Offline Test: Basic 1-hour test covering 8th-grade Maths.  
- Learning round: A personal conversation to understand the student’s motivation and background.  
- Culture fit round: Family background check, including income criteria (<5LPA)  

---

## 📅 Admission Timeline

- Applications: Open throughout the year (Rolling admissions) for fully funded campuses.  
- Application for BCA: According to UGC, tentatively close by Aug-Sep.  

---

## 🔄 Re-test Rules

If a student does not pass the entrance test on the first try or LR, they are encouraged to reapply or re-attempt LR.  

There is usually a cooling period of 10 day to study and try again.

## 🧠 Screening Test

The screening test broadly filters the students based on their aptitude for different concepts. For each program, there is a cut-off.  

The Screening Test is the first step for the student. This test is conducted both in online and offline formats. Offline seminars are conducted in regions that are remote with low internet/device availability. For example, Dantewada, Jashpur, etc. It is a paper-and-pencil test. The majority of students come through an online screening test available on the website.  

The duration of the test is 90 minutes, which is 1.5 hours.  

Online students just have to select the option and move next, for offline, they will be given an OMR sheet and which gets scanned and evaluated by the ZipGrade app.  

The passing criteria of this test to move on to the next round, which is the Learning Round is also depend on the schools that we are offering, about which you will know further in the doc in detail.  

---

## ⚠️ Things to Remember

The same paper is to be administered for the offline and online tests.  

WHY: All the students have the same/similar paper (fairness). Secondly, for the team that is taking the learning round, it becomes simple to handle one paper.  

All the sets will be different. They will be generated by the back-end.  

WHY: To avoid copying, cheating, and rote memorization of the paper.  

All the questions are going to be MCQ:  

WHY: We can do OMR only through MCQ questions. And OMR is the only way right now to do the checking of papers quickly. A paper manually would take around 20-30 minutes. OMR scanning would take less than a minute.

## 🎥 Learning Support

Students who take the Screening Test through any partner organization are provided with our Learning Video, which explains the key concepts covered in the Screening Test.  

Students who apply through “No Partner” come to NavGurukul on their own and take the test independently. For many of them, this is the first point of contact with NavGurukul.  

Once a student comes in touch with us through the Screening Test, whether they clear the test or not, we share the Learning Video with them. Students who do not clear the test can use the video to practice and re-attempt the Screening Test. Students who clear the test can use the same video to prepare better for the next stage, called the Learning Round (LR).  

The Learning Round includes questions similar to those explained in the Learning Video. More details about the Learning Round (LR) are shared later in this document.  

---

## 🔁 Learning Round

When a student attempts and clear the Screening Test, we provide a Learning Round practice video. In this video, we explain the concepts covered in the Screening Test, which will also be covered in the Learning Round. This helps the student prepare well. If the student has any doubts, they can clear them by understanding the concepts through the video.  

You might think, Why are we taking the Learning Round?, in which we are asking similar concepts and questions that the student already attempted and passed in the Screening test? The simple answer is, it is for ensuring integrity in the screening process and to be able to identify students who might have good logical thinking skills and a high effort attitude, and were close to clearing the cut-off for the priority schools, like SOP, and give them a chance.

The Future Textile Designer Programme is a residential, future-skills livelihood initiative tailored for women from highly vulnerable backgrounds—survivors of violence, trafficking, wrongful incarceration, and women living with HIV.
The programme moves beyond traditional tailoring by equipping participants with advanced textile, design, digital, and business skills that open pathways to dignified and sustainable employment.

Sustainability
Upcycling
Responsible production
Digital commerce
These fields require new technical and creative capabilities.
This programme addresses this gap by preparing women for roles in the emerging circular economy, while building long-term financial resilience and confidence.

## 💻 School of Programming (SOP): A Complete Overview
### 📝 Introduction
The School of Programming (SOP) at NavGurukul is a practical learning program designed to help students become skilled software developers from scratch.

The curriculum focuses on learning by building real projects, solving problems, and understanding how modern software systems work.

Students start with basic web development and gradually move towards advanced programming, AI integration, backend systems, and real-world projects.

The goal of SOP is to help learners:

Build strong programming fundamentals

Develop problem-solving ability

Learn modern web technologies

Build real applications

Work with AI tools

Become job-ready developers

The entire program follows a "learning by doing" approach, where students continuously practice through projects, mini-projects, and practical assignments.

### 🧠 Learning Philosophy
The School of Programming is based on a few important learning principles:

Learning by Doing: Students learn best when they build things themselves. Instead of only studying theory, learners build projects from day one.

AI as a Learning Partner: Students are encouraged to use AI tools to support their learning, helping them explore ideas, debug code, and experiment.

Anyone Can Learn Programming: The program is designed for beginners. Even students without prior coding knowledge can learn step-by-step.

Peer Learning: Students learn together by discussing problems, explaining concepts, and reviewing each other's work. Teaching others helps deepen understanding.

Daily Planning & Discipline: Students learn to manage their learning through daily planning and consistent practice.

### 🏗️ Program Structure
The SOP curriculum is divided into multiple learning phases, where each phase focuses on new concepts and technologies. Students gradually move from:
Basic Web Development → JavaScript → Backend → Databases → AI → Full-stack projects.

📅 Program Phases
🔹 Induction Phase
Duration: ~30 days

This phase prepares students for the learning journey.

Students learn: How to learn programming effectively, problem-solving mindset, number systems, flowcharts, logic building, and daily learning habits.

Goal: To build thinking ability before coding.

## 🔹 Phase 1 — HTML Foundations
Project: Student Profile & Course Portal

Students build a multi-page website using only HTML to understand structure.

Pages: Home, Profile, Courses, Feedback, Grades table, and Contact page.

Topics: HTML structure, headings, paragraphs, lists, links, images, tables, forms, and semantic HTML.

Activity: Students record videos explaining their code to demonstrate understanding.

## 🔹 Phase 2 — CSS (Styling Websites)
Students make websites visually attractive by applying CSS to the Phase 1 project.

Topics: CSS syntax, selectors, colors, fonts, spacing (margin, padding), borders, box model, and layout systems.

Modern Tools: Flexbox and CSS Grid.

Skill: Responsive design for mobile and desktop devices.

## 🔹 Phase 3 — JavaScript Fundamentals
Makes websites interactive.

Project: Interactive Quiz Application (Start quiz, dynamic questions, scoring, results, restart).

Topics: Variables, data types, operators, arrays, objects, loops, functions, conditional statements, DOM manipulation, and event handling.

## 🔹 Phase 4 — AI-Powered Content Generator
Students start working with Artificial Intelligence APIs.

They build a web application that generates content using Gemini AI.

Topics: JavaScript ES6 features, API integration, sending requests to AI models, and displaying responses.

## 🔹 Phase 5 — AI Chat Application (Node.js Backend)
Project: "Ask Gemini" Web App

Students learn backend development using Node.js and Express.js.

Topics: Backend server creation, API routes, request/response handling, and connecting frontend with backend.

## 🔹 Phase 6 — Databases
Students learn how to store and manage data using MongoDB and Mongoose.

Project: Student Feedback Manager

Topics: Database concepts, data storage, schema design, CRUD operations, and connecting backend with database.

## 🔹 Phase 7 — React Bootcamp
Students learn React, a modern frontend framework used in industry.

Topics: React components, state management, props, UI building, and frontend architecture.

## 🔹 Final Project
Students build an open-ended project (Real product, social impact solution, or a paid project).

Goal: Apply everything learned during the program.

🛠️ Skills Students Gain
Programming: JavaScript, problem solving, logic building.

Web Development: HTML, CSS, responsive design, React.

Backend Development: Node.js, Express.js, API development.

Databases: MongoDB, data management.

AI Integration: AI APIs, building AI-powered applications.

Software Engineering: Debugging, project building, explaining code, teamwork, and documentation.

## 📖 Learning Methods Used in SOP
Project Based Learning: Real projects in every phase.

Assessments: Evaluated using rubrics focusing on understanding, code structure, and communication.

### 🧾 SOF - Certificate Program in Accounting, Costing, Taxation & Digital Accounting Tools
## 📋 1. PROGRAM OVERVIEW
Program Duration: 13–15 Months (Approx. 12 months of structured learning + revision & assessments)

Eligibility: 12th Pass (Any Stream)

Pedagogical Flow: Concept → Task → Games → Case → Reflection

## 🎯 Core Objectives
Develop strong accounting and financial interpretation skills

Build advanced Excel and digital accounting tool proficiency

Enable students to read, analyze, and interpret financial statements

Develop communication, analytical, and self-learning skills

Encourage AI-assisted learning, reading of amendments, case laws, and financial news

## 🏫 Teaching Methodology
Peer-to-peer learning & Mentor–mentee learning

Case studies (corporate, NGO, MSME) & Group assignments

Simulations and games

Tool-based practical learning (Excel, Tally, Zoho Books)

## 🏆 Program Learning Outcome
By the end of the program, students will be able to:

Read and interpret financial statements of companies, firms, and non-profits

Apply accounting concepts in real-life business scenarios

Prepare and analyze financial, cost, and management reports

Use Tally and digital tools confidently

Understand tax implications for individuals, firms, and companies

## 📖 Module 1: Foundations of Financial Accounting
Objective: Build strong conceptual clarity of accounting principles and recording mechanisms.

1.1 Introduction to Accounting: Meaning, scope, objectives, advantages & limitations; Book-keeping vs accounting; Users of accounting information.

1.2 Accounting Concepts & Principles: Business entity, money measurement, going concern; Accrual, prudence, consistency, cost concept.

1.3 Accounting Terminology & Accounting Equation: Assets, liabilities, capital, drawings; Accounting equation and its applications.

1.4 Double Entry System & Journal: Golden rules of accounting; Cash and credit transactions; Journal entries from day-to-day business activities.

## 📖 Module 2: Recording, Classification & Control of Accounts
Objective: Enable students to record transactions systematically and ensure accounting accuracy.

2.1 Ledger & Balancing: Posting journal entries to ledger; Balancing of ledger accounts.

2.2 Subsidiary Books: Cash book, purchase book, sales book; Practical classification through activities and games.

2.3 Trial Balance: Meaning, objectives, preparation, and limitations.

2.4 Errors & Rectification: Types of accounting errors; Rectification of errors and suspense account.

2.5 Bank Reconciliation Statement (BRS): Cash book vs pass book; Preparation of BRS and common reconciliation items.

## 📖 Module 3: Final Accounts & Business Accounting
Objective: Develop ability to prepare and interpret financial statements of different business forms.

3.1 Final Accounts – Sole Proprietor: Trading account; Profit & loss account with adjustments; Balance sheet preparation.

3.2 Partnership Accounting: Partnership deed and profit-sharing ratio; Admission, retirement, goodwill, and revaluation.

3.3 Company Accounts: Types of companies; Share capital and basic share issue concepts; Preparation of company financial statements; Introduction to cash flow statement.

## 📖 Module 4: Management & Cost Accounting
Objective: Strengthen managerial decision-making and cost control skills.

4.1 Management Accounting Fundamentals: Nature, scope, objectives; Financial accounting vs management accounting.

4.2 Financial Statement Analysis: Comparative statements; Common size statements; Trend analysis.

4.3 Ratio Analysis: Liquidity, profitability, solvency, and activity ratios; Interpretation using real company data.

4.4 Working Capital & Capital Budgeting: Operating cycle and working capital management; Capital budgeting techniques (Payback, NPV, IRR).

4.5 Cost Accounting Basics: Classification and elements of cost; Material costing and inventory control; Labour costing and overheads; Introduction to standard costing and variance analysis.

## 📖 Module 5: Taxation – Income Tax & GST
Objective: Enable learners to understand tax laws, compute taxes, and ensure compliance.

5.1 Introduction to Income Tax: Assessee, assessment year, previous year; Residential status and scope of total income.

5.2 Heads of Income: Salary, House property, PGBP (Profits and gains of business or profession), Capital gains, and Income from other sources.

5.3 Deductions & Computation: Deductions under Chapter VI-A (80C, 80D, 80E, etc.); Step-by-step computation of total income.

5.4 Introduction to GST: Concept and objectives; Indirect taxes before GST overview; CGST, SGST, IGST meaning and applicability.

5.5 GST Compliance: Registration process and thresholds; Input Tax Credit (ITC) concept; Overview of GST returns (GSTR-1, GSTR-3B); Basic GST compliance from an accounting perspective.

## 📖 Module 6: Digital Accounting Tools – Tally Prime
Objective: Provide hands-on experience in computerized and GST-enabled accounting.

6.1 Introduction: Installation, interface, company creation, and basic configuration.

6.2 Masters Setup: Creation of ledgers and groups; GST-related ledger configuration.

6.3 Accounting Vouchers: Payment, receipt, contra, and journal vouchers; Recording GST transactions.

6.4 Reports & Reconciliation: Day book, trial balance, P&L account, balance sheet, and bank reconciliation in Tally; GST reports overview.

🏁 Overall Program Outcomes
Maintain manual and computerized books of accounts.

Prepare and analyze financial statements.

Apply cost and management accounting tools for decision-making.

Compute income tax and understand GST compliance.

Use Tally Prime confidently for accounting and GST-enabled transactions.

### GROUP: ETIOCRACY - STUDENT-LED GOVERNANCE
Etiocracy is NavGurukul’s unique system of student-led governance and distributed authority. Students take significant ownership and responsibility for daily operations, academics, and overall campus life.

1. KEY PRINCIPLES OF ETIOCRACY:
No Hierarchy, Distributed Authority: NavGurukul minimizes top-down hierarchy. Authority and responsibilities are distributed among students rather than a few individuals making all decisions.

Student-as-Partner Model: Students are active partners and contributors, not just recipients of education.

Parallel with Industry Structures: Functioning mirrors modern tech companies, using daily stand-ups, project-based work, and accountability.

2. DYNAMIC ROLES & RESPONSIBILITIES:
Short-term & Long-term Roles: Roles range from managing specific events to being long-term domain coordinators.

Multiple Roles per Partner: Students often hold multiple roles simultaneously to develop diverse skills.

Transparent Selection: Open roles have clear Job Descriptions (JDs). Selection is done through a transparent process, often involving a jury of randomly selected students for interviews.

3. STUDENT COUNCILS (DISCO):
The "Disco" teams are the prime example of Etiocracy, where student leaders manage various domains:

Domains Managed: Discipline, Placement, Academics, Facility Management, Workout, Kitchen, Life Skills, Hackathons, and English Coordination.

4. OPERATIONAL TRANSPARENCY:
Documentation: All rules, processes, and role descriptions are well-documented and accessible on platforms like Notion.

Quick Iterations: The system is agile, allowing roles and processes to be quickly adapted based on feedback and evolving campus needs.

### GROUP: STUDENT LAPTOP DISTRIBUTION POLICY (ENHANCED)
NavGurukul has introduced a deposit-waiver policy to support students who face financial challenges in paying the standard ₹15,000 security deposit for taking laptops off-campus.

1. ELIGIBILITY & GOAL:
Target: Students with genuine financial challenges who cannot arrange the deposit.

Goal: To ensure learning continues without interruption for deserving students.

2. REQUIREMENTS FOR DEPOSIT WAIVER:
Legal Bond: The student must sign a legal bond promising to return the laptop on time and in good condition.

Guardians: If the student is under 18, parents must also sign the document.

5 Guarantors: The student must find 5 other students to act as guarantors who agree to ensure the laptop is returned.

Costs: Students must cover the cost of the stamp paper and any courier charges if they cannot return to campus personally.

3. BOND PAPER VALUES BY STATE:
Himachal Pradesh (Dharamshala, Baru Sahib): ₹100

Rajasthan (Udaipur): ₹200

Chhattisgarh (Raipur, Jashpur, Dantewada): ₹250

Bihar (Kishanganj): ₹300

Maharashtra (Pune): ₹500

Karnataka (Sarjapur, Bangalore): ₹500

4. CONSEQUENCES OF VIOLATION:
Legal Action: Failure to return the laptop on time or in good condition will result in legal action, including filing an FIR.

Trust Clause: If the overall compliance rate falls below 99%, the waiver option will be withdrawn for everyone.

Guarantor Penalty: If guarantors fail to help retrieve a laptop, they lose eligibility for the waiver and cannot act as guarantors for one year.

5. STUDENT EXPECTATIONS (OFF-CAMPUS):
Students taking laptops home must:

Continue working toward set learning goals.

Participate in hackathons and join daily stand-up meetings.

Join eligible placement activities.

Failure to meet these will result in an immediate recall of the laptop.

### GROUP: STUDENT LEAVE POLICY
NavGurukul's leave policy for students balances academic engagement with personal needs.

1. ATTENDANCE & PUNCTUALITY:
Core Rule: Mandatory daily attendance for 8 hours of coding and 1 hour of English practice.

Daily Schedule: Punctuality is strictly enforced for all sessions, meal times, and campus duties.

2. LEAVE ENTITLEMENT (NEW):
Graduated Students: 40 leaves per year.

Students Pursuing Studies (Graduation/Other): 70 leaves per year to accommodate exams and external studies.

3. APPLICATION PROCESS:
Short Absences (1-2 Days): Notify Academic or Life Skill Associates via Slack/form for verbal or written approval.

Extended Leave (3+ Days): Formal written request required with reasons (e.g., medical certificate). Requires approval from campus leadership/directors.

4. CONSEQUENCES OF UNAPPROVED ABSENCE:
Academic & Disciplinary: Can lead to warnings, project delays, or point deductions in the energy reward system.
