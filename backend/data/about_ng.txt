### GROUP: ABOUT NAVGURUKUL (OVERVIEW & MISSION)
NavGurukul is a technology-led non-profit organization established in 2016 with a mission to enable underserved youth across India to access dignified, future-ready careers. Founded on the radical belief that "Talent is universal, but opportunity is not," the organization works to dismantle structural barriers that prevent millions of young people from realizing their potential. By designing inclusive and scalable systems, NavGurukul provides high-quality, affordable, and outcomes-driven learning pathways that prepare students—often first-generation learners—for the growing digital economy.

- Core Purpose: Bridges the divide between potential and opportunity through industry-relevant education and skilling.
- Strategic Methods: Promotes digital literacy, quality higher education, and improved employment access.
- Long-term Goal: To create a nationwide network where education and skills are inclusive and accessible to every motivated student.
- Official website link of Navgurukul - https://www.navgurukul.org/




## 🎓 BCA Programme Fee

The BCA programme has a one-time fee of ₹23500.  

Students only need to pay this one-time amount, and the rest of the learning support is provided through NavGurukul.  

The fee component is included to ensure the commitment of the students. If a student drops out midway through the course, the seat remains vacant for the entire academic year, since this is a degree program. Unlike diploma or certificate programs, where admissions can happen on a rolling basis, degree program seats cannot be filled once the academic year has started.

## 🚀 Selection Process

- Online/Offline Test: Basic 1-hour test covering 8th-grade Maths.  
- Learning round: A personal conversation to understand the student’s motivation and background.  
- Culture fit round: Family background check, including income criteria (<5LPA)

### GROUP: SAMA - REPURPOSING E-WASTE INTO LEARNING TOOLS
Sama is a NavGurukul initiative that turns e-waste (specifically aged laptops) into refurbished devices to bridge the digital divide for underserved youth and women across India. It provides a sustainable pathway for organizations to donate end-of-life hardware that would otherwise end up in landfills, contributing to pollution.

#### THE PROCESS (HOW SAMA WORKS):
- Collection: Corporate partners donate their end-of-life laptops instead of discarding them.
- Refurbishment: Devices undergo secure data erasure, hardware upgrades, and are pre-loaded with educational content.
- Distribution: Refurbished devices are shared through verified NGOs and institutions to reach women and youth in remote areas.
- Impact Tracking: An in-house "Install RMS" system monitors real-time usage and educational outcomes ethically.

#### IMPACT IN NUMBERS:
- Students Impacted: 2,39,730 students reached, including 1,19,865 girls across 1,471 schools in India.
- Digital Labs: 47 labs established in Karnataka, benefiting 7,845 students.
- Skills Training: 3,615 individuals trained in digital literacy, design, data entry, and coding.
- Higher Education: 6 students have advanced to master’s programs through NGO collaborations.

### GROUP: Meraki 
Meraki is an important initiative by NavGurukul that focuses on making programming and digital skills accessible, especially to learners from underserved communities. It's designed to be a readiness program that can open doors to job opportunities or further training in the IT sector.

Here's a breakdown of what Meraki entails:

*   Online Learning Platform: Meraki is a mobile-first platform, primarily an Android application, that allows students to learn coding and digital skills using just a smartphone and an optional keyboard. It's part of NavGurukul Foundation for Social Welfare and began in 2020 to offer low-cost online tech education.
*   Introductory Courses: It offers a free, 3-month online introductory course on Software Programming (primarily Python) and Touch Typing. Other courses include JavaScript, Scratch, web development, and spoken English.
*   Target Audience: Meraki specifically focuses on young women and girls to address the underrepresentation of women in the IT sector. It also aims to reach students from secondary schools and low-income families across India.
*   Learning Approach: The platform encourages self-learning, providing course content, live classes, and expert mentoring. It also offers content in multiple regional languages to ensure clarity and accessibility, while also developing English skills.
*   Pathway to Further Opportunities: Meraki acts as a stepping stone. Successfully completing the program can lead to job opportunities or admission into more aspirational training programs, including NavGurukul's fully-funded residential software engineering program.
*   Impact: The Meraki app is used by over 55,000 learners across various states, providing them with foundational tech skills. NavGurukul's vision includes reaching 100 million remote Meraki users to train them in software programming and data typing in the next five years.
MERAKI MOBILE APP: 
Meraki brings programming to young adults across India, aligned with NEP 2020.

Courses: Scratch, Web Development, Typing, Python, and English Speaking.

Features: Live classes, vernacular curriculum, offline practice, and portfolio creation.

Impact: 68.67% retention rate with average sessions of ~28 minutes.

Etiocracy is NavGurukul’s unique system of student-led governance and distributed authority. Students take significant ownership and responsibility for daily operations, academics, and overall campus life.

1. KEY PRINCIPLES OF ETIOCRACY:
No Hierarchy, Distributed Authority: NavGurukul minimizes top-down hierarchy. Authority and responsibilities are distributed among students rather than a few individuals making all decisions.

Student-as-Partner Model: Students are active partners and contributors, not just recipients of education.

Parallel with Industry Structures: Functioning mirrors modern tech companies, using daily stand-ups, project-based work, and accountability.

2. DYNAMIC ROLES & RESPONSIBILITIES:
Short-term & Long-term Roles: Roles range from managing specific events to being long-term domain coordinators.

Multiple Roles per Partner: Students often hold multiple roles simultaneously to develop diverse skills.

Transparent Selection: Open roles have clear Job Descriptions (JDs). Selection is done through a transparent process, often involving a jury of randomly selected students for interviews.

3. STUDENT COUNCILS (DISCO):
The "Disco" teams are the prime example of Etiocracy, where student leaders manage various domains:

Domains Managed: Discipline, Placement, Academics, Facility Management, Workout, Kitchen, Life Skills, Hackathons, and English Coordination.

4. OPERATIONAL TRANSPARENCY:
Documentation: All rules, processes, and role descriptions are well-documented and accessible on platforms like Notion.

Quick Iterations: The system is agile, allowing roles and processes to be quickly adapted based on feedback and evolving campus needs.

### GROUP: STUDENT LAPTOP DISTRIBUTION POLICY (ENHANCED)
NavGurukul has introduced a deposit-waiver policy to support students who face financial challenges in paying the standard ₹15,000 security deposit for taking laptops off-campus.

1. ELIGIBILITY & GOAL:
Target: Students with genuine financial challenges who cannot arrange the deposit.

Goal: To ensure learning continues without interruption for deserving students.

2. REQUIREMENTS FOR DEPOSIT WAIVER:
Legal Bond: The student must sign a legal bond promising to return the laptop on time and in good condition.

Guardians: If the student is under 18, parents must also sign the document.

5 Guarantors: The student must find 5 other students to act as guarantors who agree to ensure the laptop is returned.

Costs: Students must cover the cost of the stamp paper and any courier charges if they cannot return to campus personally.

3. BOND PAPER VALUES BY STATE:
Himachal Pradesh (Dharamshala, Baru Sahib): ₹100

Rajasthan (Udaipur): ₹200

Chhattisgarh (Raipur, Jashpur, Dantewada): ₹250

Bihar (Kishanganj): ₹300

Maharashtra (Pune): ₹500

Karnataka (Sarjapur, Bangalore): ₹500

4. CONSEQUENCES OF VIOLATION:
Legal Action: Failure to return the laptop on time or in good condition will result in legal action, including filing an FIR.

Trust Clause: If the overall compliance rate falls below 99%, the waiver option will be withdrawn for everyone.

Guarantor Penalty: If guarantors fail to help retrieve a laptop, they lose eligibility for the waiver and cannot act as guarantors for one year.

5. STUDENT EXPECTATIONS (OFF-CAMPUS):
Students taking laptops home must:

Continue working toward set learning goals.

Participate in hackathons and join daily stand-up meetings.

Join eligible placement activities.

Failure to meet these will result in an immediate recall of the laptop.

### GROUP: STUDENT LEAVE POLICY
NavGurukul's leave policy for students balances academic engagement with personal needs.

1. ATTENDANCE & PUNCTUALITY:
Core Rule: Mandatory daily attendance for 8 hours of coding and 1 hour of English practice.

Daily Schedule: Punctuality is strictly enforced for all sessions, meal times, and campus duties.

2. LEAVE ENTITLEMENT (NEW):
Graduated Students: 40 leaves per year.
